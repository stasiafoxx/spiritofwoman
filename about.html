About Spirit of Woman

Since 1997, Spirit Of Woman has provided and managed treatment programs for pregnant and parenting women in need of substance abuse intervention. Spirit Of Woman provides this type of support through inpatient aftercare, and sober living programs. From 1997 to 2000, Spirit Of Woman leased facilities through another organization that served as its fiscal agent. In 2000, the organization was incorporated as a nonprofit and leased a 24-unit apartment complex that served over 30 women and 75 children daily. In 2004, Spirit Of Woman relocated to its current site where it can provide inpatient services for 70 women and 105 children, making it one of the primary residential substance abuse treatment facilities for women with children in Fresno County.

Audrey Riley, the founder and current Executive Director of Spirit of Woman, was named one of the Top Ten Business and Professional Women of the Year for 2003. Ms. Riley received this prestigious award in recognition of her accomplishments in developing and implementing a successful substance abuse treatment program that continues to serve women and their children throughout Fresno County. On January 31 of 2002, Ms. Riley was honored with the Temple Beth Israel Social Action Award.
